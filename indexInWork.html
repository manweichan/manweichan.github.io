Academics

I'm a PhD candidate in Aerospace Engineering and NSF Fellow at MIT. For my Master's degree, I developed a guidance algorithm to allow satellites to rendezvous and dock with an uncontrolled tumbling object. For my PhD, I am studying the reduction of data latency in remote sensing satellite constellation. I hope to design systems and inform the aerospace industry of constellation architectures that will provide first responders with the situational awareness needed to tackle any unforseen disasater.

Professional

I dream that one day space, and the experiences that accompany it, are not only available to governments and the ultra-wealthy. I've worked with various Aerospace companies to develop the commercial space industry in pursuit of this goal. With NanoRacks, I wrote proposals to both utilize the International Space Station and to build an private space station, with Orbit Fab, I analyzed an array of sensors and low-power communication for rendezvous and docking, and with stealth-mode start-up, I built and wrote space hardware and software to build a constellation to connect the traditionally unconnected. Space For All

I founded the MIT Space Seminar, a speaker series that brings experts in the industry to the MIT community. Past speakers included C-suite execs such as Natalya Bailey and Andrew Rush, former astronauts such as Tony Antonelli, and indsutry veterans such as Pete Worden. During my tenure as the president of SEDS at MIT, I organized outreach initaitves to build dry ice rockets for middle school girls with the MIT society of women engineers and hosted MIT visits for the Eagle X Robotics Team from the Monterrey Institute of Technology in Mexico. I've also been a part of the Space Generation Fusion Forum Organizing Team in 2020, 2021, and 2022, putting together a high-profile conference for an international audience.

International

I lived for a year in the Atacama Desert in Chile, working as a telescope technician for the CLASS telescope on Cerro Toco (8000 meters above sea level). During the day, I tended to the telescope and at night I conducted astronomy tours for tourists from all over the world. Before that, I also worked at CERN, living in Geneva, Switzerland, and working with an optical experiment that hoped to study the hypothetical chameleon particle.
